## A Study of 1,100 Employees Found That Remote Workers Feel Shunned and Left Out

by Joseph [Grenny](https://hbr.org/search?term=Joseph%20Grenny) and David [Maxfield](https://hbr.org/search?term=David%20Maxfield)

November 2, 2017

![](_page_0_Picture_4.jpeg)

Summary. New research shows that remote employees are more likely to feel left out and ganged up on than their on-site colleagues. Specifically, they worry that coworkers say bad things behind their backs, make changes to projects... more

When it comes to virtual teams, if you're out of sight, you're also out of mind. While more and more people are working [remotely](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/02/15/us/remote-workers-work-from-home.html), our recent study suggests that unless we take extra measures to build trust and connection with colleagues, we pay dearly for doing so.
