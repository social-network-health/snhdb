# Why Open-Source Developers Are Burning Out

Open-source was supposed to be a barn-raising. It turned into unpaid labor by stressed-out individuals

![](_page_0_Picture_3.jpeg)

Clive [Thompson](https://medium.com/@clivethompson?source=post_page---byline--1a860854884c---------------------------------------) · [Follow](https://medium.com/m/signin?actionUrl=https%3A%2F%2Fmedium.com%2F_%2Fsubscribe%2Fuser%2Fd4352eb68115&operation=register&redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fmedium.com%2Fbetter-programming%2Fwhy-open-source-developers-are-burning-out-1a860854884c&user=Clive+Thompson&userId=d4352eb68115&source=post_page-d4352eb68115--byline--1a860854884c---------------------post_header------------------) Published in Better [Programming](https://medium.com/better-programming?source=post_page---byline--1a860854884c---------------------------------------) 9 min read · Jan 13, 2022

There's a software library called "colors", which does one simple thing: It lets programmers change the color of text in Node apps.

It's quite popular; around 19,000 software projects [currently](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/dev-corrupts-npm-libs-colors-and-faker-breaking-thousands-of-apps/) use it, and it has over 20 million weekly [downloads](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/dev-corrupts-npm-libs-colors-and-faker-breaking-thousands-of-apps/) from people trying it out.

But recently, anyone using "colors" in their app got a shock when they updated that software library. Their screens all went haywire — producing the stream of nonsense text you see above.

What happened? Well, Marak Squires, the software developer for "colors", [had](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/dev-corrupts-npm-libs-colors-and-faker-breaking-thousands-of-apps/) [intentionally](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/dev-corrupts-npm-libs-colors-and-faker-breaking-thousands-of-apps/) sabotaged his own library of code. He added an infinite loop that generated gibberish characters, utterly b0rking the functionality of "colors".

And … why would he do that? It's not fully clear; he may, as observers have noted, been having any number of [mental-health](https://gizmodo.com/an-open-source-developer-just-caused-a-whole-lot-of-cha-1848331944) issues.

But one thing Squires had recently made clear is that he was feeling burned out
